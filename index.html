Why do you need a cat?

Reducing stress and anxiety. Studies have shown that people who regularly pet and play with cats reduce their stress levels and receive a dose of the “tenderness and happiness hormone” oxytocin. This, among other things, reduces the risk of cardiovascular diseases. Help combat allergies It may seem strange, but research suggests that children raised with cats are less likely to develop not only allergic reactions to dust and pets, but also food allergies. Apparently, this is a consequence of some kind of training of the immune system.Improved physical health. Playing with and caring for your cat, like adding a coin to your daily physical activity treasury, can help maintain physical fitness, metabolism and reduce the risk of developing heart and vascular diseases. Getting rid of feelings of loneliness. Cats are excellent companions, including for lonely people. Their presence can help combat feelings of loneliness and isolation, which can lead to depression, especially in older people.
